# Portfolio Context

Shared language for the portfolio content model and the AI assistant that explains it.

## Language

**Showcase Order**:
The editorial ranking used to present projects by importance in portfolio lists and AI answers. Lower positions appear before higher positions.
_Avoid_: Importance, priority, weight

**Answer Lens**:
The user intent used by the AI assistant to adjust which projects or experiences it emphasizes in an answer. Showcase Order is the default, but an Answer Lens can boost a project when it better matches the question. The main lenses are AI, Full-stack/Product, Cloud/DevOps, Mobile, and Learning/Personal.
_Avoid_: Mode, category, route

**Project**:
A shipped or portfolio-worthy body of work that can appear in lists, detail pages, and AI answers.
_Avoid_: Case, item

**Hotel Operations Platform**:
The canonical product category for Brixa. Use it when describing Brixa as broader than a booking-only tool and more specific than a generic hotel management system.
_Avoid_: Hotel booking system, hotel management system

**Guardrail Tone**:
The assistant's tone when redirecting out-of-scope questions. Normal off-topic inputs get a professional redirect; clearly weird or silly inputs may get a warm, lightly playful redirect.
_Avoid_: Sarcasm, mockery, passive aggression

**Production AI Experience**:
Hands-on work building AI features in production systems. For this portfolio, Brixa is the primary proof point.
_Avoid_: Machine learning, model training, AI research

**AI-Native Full-Stack Developer**:
Marcos's canonical professional title, combining two equal pillars: AI product building and full-stack development. His ownership spans product interfaces, backend services, AI workflows, and infrastructure.
_Avoid_: AI engineer, software engineer, generic full-stack developer, AI researcher

**AI-Native**:
A product-building approach in which AI is a core product capability rather than a decorative integration. It includes designing agent workflows, tool use, prompts, backend services, interfaces, and infrastructure needed to operate AI features in production.
_Avoid_: AI-powered as a vague label, model training, AI research

**AI Product Builder**:
One of the two equal pillars of Marcos's professional identity, alongside Full-Stack Development. It means turning AI capabilities into useful production products.
_Avoid_: AI expert, AI specialist

**Full-Stack Development**:
One of the two equal pillars of Marcos's professional identity, alongside AI Product Builder. It means owning production work across interfaces, backend services, data, integrations, deployment, and infrastructure.
_Avoid_: Supporting skill, frontend-and-backend only

**Messaging Hierarchy**:
Lead with AI Product Builder and Full-Stack Development as equal pillars. Present mobile and cloud infrastructure as supporting evidence of full-stack breadth rather than competing top-level identities.
_Avoid_: Listing AI, full-stack, mobile, cloud, and product as equal headline labels

**Experience Signal**:
Use "4+ years shipping software" as the compact homepage expression of Marcos's professional experience.
_Avoid_: 4+ years shipping, 4+ years building production products

**Portfolio Voice**:
Confident, direct, and technically precise. Communicate through shipped work, ownership, and outcomes instead of broad adjectives or trend language.
_Avoid_: Passionate developer, cutting-edge AI, AI expert, latest technologies

**CV Variant Positioning**:
Use "Full-Stack Developer" as the header title for the master/full-stack CV, with "AI product builder" in the opening profile sentence. Use "AI Product Builder / Full-Stack Developer" as the header title for the AI-oriented CV variant.
_Avoid_: Making every CV variant use the same headline, hiding AI capability from the full-stack CV

**Master CV Rocking Product Structure**:
Use compact bullets under Rocking Product that show full-stack product breadth, Brixa production AI workflows, and Claimence cloud infrastructure ownership. Mention Brixa and Claimence by name without creating project subsections in the master/full-stack CV.
_Avoid_: Generic Rocking Product bullets, machine learning, turning the master CV into the AI variant

**AI CV Rocking Product Structure**:
Use Brixa as the dominant Rocking Product proof point in the AI-oriented CV. Most bullets should cover Brixa's production AI workflows and business/product impact, with one supporting bullet for broader full-stack, mobile, cloud, or CI/CD delivery.
_Avoid_: Making the AI CV a generic full-stack CV, burying Brixa, claiming model training or AI research

**Hero Positioning**:
Use "AI product builder." and "Full-stack developer." as two deliberately separate headline lines. The equal visual treatment reinforces that AI product building and full-stack development are equal professional pillars.
_Avoid_: Uncontrolled headline wrapping, I build, making either role visually secondary

**Hero Supporting Copy**:
Use "Building production AI systems and full-stack software across interfaces, backend services, and infrastructure."
_Avoid_: Generic technology lists, mobile and cloud as competing headline identities

**Hero Signals**:
Use "4+ years shipping software", "Production AI systems", and "Web / Mobile / Cloud", in that order, as the compact homepage credibility signals.
_Avoid_: 4+ years shipping, AI / Cloud, repeating Full-stack developer

**Homepage Narrative Order**:
Keep the existing order: hero positioning, interactive portfolio AI assistant, selected projects, then capability-led skills. The assistant acts as an immediate demonstration of AI-native product thinking.
_Avoid_: Moving projects ahead of the assistant, treating the assistant as an unrelated widget

**Assistant Visible Copy**:
Keep the current homepage assistant introduction unchanged: "Trained on this portfolio. Ask about projects, stack, experience, or whether I am available for the next build."
_Avoid_: Rewriting this introduction as part of the positioning update

**Capability-Led Skills**:
The homepage skills model groups expertise by what Marcos can build: AI Product Systems, Full-Stack Development, Mobile Development, and Cloud & Infrastructure. Concrete technologies remain supporting evidence within those capabilities.
_Avoid_: Stack inventory, vendor-led identity, separating frontend, backend, and databases as unrelated abilities

**Visible AI Skills**:
The visible AI Product Systems skill group should include capability labels first, then supporting implementation tools including OpenAI API and Claude API. Claude API may appear as text even if no logo asset exists yet.
_Avoid_: Hiding Claude API from the website, making AI skills only a logo carousel

**Assistant AI Evidence**:
The portfolio assistant should answer technical AI questions with the confirmed Production AI Practices plus OpenAI API and Claude API as implementation tools.
_Avoid_: Limiting assistant answers to generic AI workflows, omitting Claude API, overstating unsupported model-training expertise

**Education Visibility**:
Keep education providers and learning sources, including Anthropic Courses, in CV education and assistant answers about learning background. Do not surface them in the primary homepage narrative.
_Avoid_: Leading the portfolio with learning sources instead of shipped work

**AI Implementation Tools**:
OpenAI API, Claude API, LangChain, and Vercel AI SDK are supporting technologies within AI Product Systems. They do not define or substitute for Marcos's production AI capabilities.
_Avoid_: Presenting AI expertise as a list of SDKs, attributing LangChain or Vercel AI SDK to Brixa

**Agent Lifecycle Hooks**:
Pre-hooks and post-hooks that run around model calls, tool execution, delegation, or other agent workflow stages. They provide controlled extension points for observing or modifying agent behavior.
_Avoid_: Hooks, React hooks

**Production AI Practices**:
Marcos's transferable AI system skill set: agents, tool calling, structured outputs, prompt design, context engineering, evaluations, guardrails, human-in-the-loop workflows, and Agent Lifecycle Hooks. Brixa is the primary production proof point, not the boundary of this expertise.
_Avoid_: Latest AI things, general AI expertise, experimental-only skills

**Brixa Business Impact**:
Brixa's AI workflows helped increase booking conversions and contributed to higher sales while reducing repetitive guest communication and operational work. Describe the observed direction of impact without publishing unavailable figures or attributing the result solely to AI.
_Avoid_: Guaranteed revenue growth, invented percentages, AI directly caused all sales growth

**Primary Portfolio Audience**:
Product-oriented companies considering Marcos for AI-native full-stack development, especially recruiters and technical leads. Portfolio messaging leads with product and business outcomes, then supports them with concrete technical depth.
_Avoid_: General audience, AI research teams, technology-only positioning

**Project Case Study**:
An evidence-driven project narrative organized around the product, Marcos's role, business or user impact, and technical work. Major professional projects receive fuller case studies; smaller projects may use a lighter version.
_Avoid_: Project database record, technology list, uniform project template

**Case Study Portfolio**:
The four full Project Case Studies are Brixa for AI product building and full-stack ownership, Travel Booking App for conventional full-stack product delivery, Grab & Eat for mobile and full-stack breadth, and Claimence for cloud and infrastructure ownership. KeySwap and personal projects remain lighter supporting entries.
_Avoid_: Giving every project equal depth, treating personal projects as primary proof

**Brixa Compact Description**:
Use a short homepage/card description that names Brixa as an AI hotel operations platform, mentions automated guest communication and booking workflows, and includes the conversion/repetitive-work impact signal.
_Avoid_: Only saying it answers guest questions, calling it just a booking system

**Brixa Technical Depth**:
For Brixa's Project Case Study, explicitly mention production AI workflows using agents, tool calling, structured outputs, context engineering, evaluations, guardrails, human-in-the-loop flows, and Agent Lifecycle Hooks.
_Avoid_: Latest AI things, AI magic, model training, AI research

**Visible Delegation**:
A user-facing trace showing that the portfolio agent understood and routed a question to a specialist capability. It may reveal lifecycle status, specialist identity, tool names, durations, and sources, but never private reasoning or chain-of-thought.
_Avoid_: Thought process, chain-of-thought, internal reasoning

**Routing Profile**:
The structured classification of a visitor request by scope, Answer Lens, and complexity. A `general` lens confidently requires no Specialist, while `unknown` explicitly abstains and leaves delegation to the Orchestrator.
_Avoid_: Single intent, mode

**Confidence-Gated Routing**:
The delegation policy where high-confidence Routing Profiles invoke Specialists deterministically, while ambiguous or low-confidence requests let the Orchestrator select Specialist tools. Complexity may select multiple Specialists, but does not by itself require agent-chosen routing.
_Avoid_: Fully deterministic routing, unrestricted agent routing

**Delegation Budget**:
The per-turn limit allowing each of the four Specialists to run at most once. Independent Specialist work may run in parallel, and repeated delegation to the same Specialist is not allowed within a turn.
_Avoid_: Retry loop, unlimited tool calls

**Portfolio Agent**:
The visitor-facing AI system that explains Marcos's portfolio through classification, evidence retrieval, Specialist delegation, and Orchestrator synthesis. This is the canonical technical name; display branding may differ.
_Avoid_: Chatbot, ChatBot, Fitz

**Orchestrator**:
The portfolio agent that owns the conversation and every final answer. It may answer directly or delegate bounded research to a Specialist.
_Avoid_: Main subagent, master agent

**Specialist**:
A lens-aligned agent that returns focused, grounded findings to the Orchestrator. The initial Specialists cover AI, Product/Full-stack, Cloud/DevOps, and Mobile; they never answer the visitor directly.
_Avoid_: Topic chatbot, project agent

**Conversation Window**:
The latest eight validated user and assistant messages supplied as conversational context. Older messages are omitted rather than summarized.
_Avoid_: Full history, conversation summary

**Conversation Retention**:
The 90-day period during which anonymous conversations and their operational traces remain available for restoration, debugging, and evaluation before scheduled deletion.
_Avoid_: Permanent history, indefinite retention

## Example Dialogue

Dev: Should the project list use database id order?

Domain expert: No, use Showcase Order. Claimence and Brixa should appear before older or smaller work because they carry more professional signal.

Dev: Should Claimence always appear before Grab & Eat because it has AWS and Terraform?

Domain expert: No. Grab & Eat has the stronger default Showcase Order because it demonstrates a full product across mobile, web, and backend. Claimence moves ahead only when the Answer Lens is cloud, DevOps, AWS, or Terraform.

Dev: Should the assistant say Marcos has AI experience?

Domain expert: Yes. Say he has Production AI Experience through Brixa, where he works on AI agents, tool-using workflows, prompt-driven automation, and OpenAI API integrations. Do not call it machine learning.

Dev: Should weird out-of-scope inputs get the same response as normal off-topic questions?

Domain expert: No. Keep normal redirects professional, but use a lightly playful Guardrail Tone for clearly weird or silly inputs.
